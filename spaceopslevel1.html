Phase 5: Reaching Low Earth Orbit

Mission Task

After igniting the engines and beginning your ascent, your goal is to guide the rocket safely into Low Earth Orbit (LEO). Follow the steps below to ensure a successful orbit.

Guiding the Rocket to Orbit

  • Step 1: Monitor your **altitude** and **velocity** during the ascent. The rocket should climb straight up until reaching around **10km altitude**.
  • Step 2: At **10km**, begin your **gravity turn** by gradually tilting the rocket **eastward (90 degrees on the Navball)**. This will help you achieve orbit.
  • Step 3: Continue the burn until your **apoapsis** (highest point of your orbit) reaches between **80-100km**. Once reached, press **X** to cut the engines.

Circularizing the Orbit

  • Step 4: When you reach **apoapsis**, point your rocket **prograde** (green circle with a dot on the Navball) and start burning again to circularize your orbit.
  • Step 5: Watch the **periapsis** (lowest point of your orbit) rise. Keep burning until both apoapsis and periapsis are above **70km**, ensuring a stable Low Earth Orbit.

Satellite Deployment

  • Step 6: Once you're in orbit, activate the final stage by pressing **Spacebar** to deploy the satellite.
  • Step 7: If necessary, adjust the satellite's position using the **RCS (Reaction Control System)** by pressing **R** to fine-tune its orbit.
