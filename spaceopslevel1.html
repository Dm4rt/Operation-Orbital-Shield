Space Operations - Level 1

In this level, you will use Kerbal Space Program (KSP) to simulate launching a rocket carrying a satellite into orbit. Follow the steps below to achieve a successful launch and deployment.

Step 1: Open **Kerbal Space Program** and enter the Vehicle Assembly Building (VAB). Begin by selecting a **command module** that will serve as the core of your rocket.

Step 2: Attach a **fuel tank** and **engine** to your command module. Ensure you have enough fuel to reach orbit. You can choose from different stages to improve efficiency during different phases of the launch.

Step 3: Add **decouplers** between each stage to separate different parts of the rocket as they run out of fuel. For the satellite, attach it to the final stage with a decoupler so it can be deployed once you're in orbit.

Step 4: After building your rocket, proceed to the **launch pad**. **Throttle up** your engines to 100% and activate your first stage to begin the launch.

Step 5: As your rocket ascends, keep an eye on your **altitude** and **velocity**. Once you're above 10km, begin to tilt your rocket eastward to start your gravity turn, which helps achieve orbit.

Step 6: When you reach an **apoapsis** of around 80-100km, cut your engines and coast to apoapsis. Then, perform a **prograde burn** to circularize your orbit.

Step 7: Once in orbit, deploy your satellite by activating the final stage. Use the satellite's **reaction control system** (RCS) or **engines** to adjust its position if necessary.

Step 8: Monitor the satellite's orbit and ensure it's stable. Congratulations, you've successfully deployed your satellite!
