Mission Brief: Operation Orbital Shield

As part of a critical military mission, you are tasked with ensuring the successful launch and sustained operational security of a strategic satellite. This satellite will provide essential communications, surveillance, and intelligence support to national defense efforts. Your mission is divided into two phases, covering both the satellite's deployment and its protection from adversarial threats.

There are two key objectives:

  1. Phase 1: Satellite Command and Launch: In the initial phase, your primary responsibility is to design and launch a satellite into Low Earth Orbit (LEO). You must ensure precise rocket configuration and successful deployment to achieve optimal orbital parameters for long-term stability and functionality.
  2. Phase 2: Space Defense and Threat Response: After the satellite is operational, it will be exposed to various threats, including electromagnetic interference, jamming, and potential adversarial attacks. Your role is to monitor, protect, and defend the satellite by employing electromagnetic warfare (EMW) tactics. This involves tuning communication frequencies, deploying countermeasures, and ensuring encrypted and secure transmissions.

Plan and execute your mission successfully to maintain vital communication and intelligence systems for national security.
